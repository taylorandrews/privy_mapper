Process







Data

The data was collected by Privy from multiple listing service (MLS) online. The organization of this data is a big part of the sevice that they provide. I took the data in the greater DC/Northern Virginia/Maryland area that was already classified manually by Privy. After removing points with missing data, there were about 250,000 homes in the data. My goal was to emulate the geographic clusters, and make them even more homogenous by bringing in more features from the MLS dataset.

Modeling

I utilized the agglomerative clustering model from scikit-learn. This model starts by taking the 'closest' two data points and combining them into the same group. It then treats the center of those points as a new point, or cluster centroid. It then finds the next two closest points and combines them and so on, until there are n clusters remaining. For n, I used the same number of clusters per zip code in the dataset.













Results

After training my model scoring system to preform very well with the pre-labeled zones from Privy, I let the agglomerative clustering algorithm loose on the data. I ended up with clusters that somewhat emulated the geographically clustered houses from Privy. My clusters overlapped somewhat, to combine houses that are a little further apart, but have features in common with other houses in their new cluster.
